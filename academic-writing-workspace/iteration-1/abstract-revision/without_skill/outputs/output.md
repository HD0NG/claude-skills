Accurate digital terrain models (DTMs) are essential for flood risk assessment, urban planning, and hydrological modeling, yet their derivation from airborne LiDAR point clouds remains challenging in densely vegetated and built-up areas. Existing filtering-based approaches depend on auxiliary inputs — such as land cover classifications or intensity data — that are frequently unavailable or inconsistent across acquisition campaigns. We address this gap by training a Residual U-Net solely on gridded digital surface models (DSMs) from the AHN4 nationwide LiDAR survey of the Netherlands, eliminating the need for ancillary data. On a held-out test set spanning diverse urban, suburban, and vegetated terrain, the model reduces mean absolute error by X% relative to the leading conventional baseline while preserving sharp break-lines in complex building environments. These results demonstrate that deep learning can reliably recover bare-earth surfaces from a single DSM input, offering a scalable pathway for automated DTM generation from current and future high-density LiDAR acquisitions.
