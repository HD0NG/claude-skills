Accurate digital terrain models (DTMs) derived from airborne laser scanning are essential for flood risk assessment, urban planning, and hydrological modelling, yet existing ground-filtering methods degrade in performance over complex urban scenes and dense vegetation without auxiliary inputs such as full-waveform data or manually tuned parameters. We propose a Residual U-Net trained end-to-end to predict bare-earth elevation directly from digital surface models (DSMs), requiring no additional sensor data or hand-crafted rules. The model was trained and evaluated on the Actueel Hoogtebestand Nederland 4 (AHN4) dataset, which provides nationwide airborne laser scanning coverage of the Netherlands at 0.5 m resolution.
